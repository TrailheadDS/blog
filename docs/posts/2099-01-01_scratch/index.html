Maps are great visualization tools for presentation. I asked ChatGPT why maps are good at displaying data this is what it said:

Maps are a powerful tool for displaying data because they allow for the visualization of spatial relationships and patterns. By presenting data on a map, it becomes possible to see geographic trends and patterns that might not be apparent when viewing the data in a table or chart. Here are some reasons why maps are good at displaying data:

Geographic context: Maps provide a visual representation of geographic areas, allowing viewers to see the location of data points in relation to one another. This context can provide important insights into the distribution of data and help to identify patterns and trends.

Spatial patterns: Maps can be used to show spatial patterns in data, such as clusters or hotspots of activity. This can be especially useful for analyzing data related to population density, traffic patterns, or other geographic phenomena.

Simplification: Maps can simplify complex data by presenting it in a visual format that is easy to understand. For example, a map of the United States that shows the prevalence of a disease by state can be easier to interpret than a table of raw data.

Interactive features: With the rise of digital mapping tools, maps can now include interactive features such as zooming, panning, and hovering over data points to reveal additional information. These features can enhance the viewer’s ability to explore and analyze the data.

Communication: Maps are a universal language and can be easily understood by people of different cultures and backgrounds. This makes them an effective tool for communicating data to a broad audience.

Overall, maps are good at displaying data because they provide a powerful visual tool for analyzing spatial relationships and patterns. They can simplify complex data, provide important context, and communicate information in a way that is easy to understand.
